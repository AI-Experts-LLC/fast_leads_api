## Email Template Philosophy

### Core Principles Applied

Based on "Cold Emailing 101" best practices:

1. **Goal: Get a Response** (not book a meeting, not close a deal)
2. **Personalization at Scale**: Balance manual research with automation
3. **Three-Touch Series**: Intro ‚Üí Follow-up ‚Üí Breakup
4. **Under 100 Words**: Brief, focused, and buyer-centric
5. **Pattern Interruption**: Stand out from generic outreach
6. **Open-Ended Questions**: Easy yes/no responses
7. **Show Me You Know Me¬Æ**: Reference specific, relevant details

### Metrus-Specific Enhancements

From the Personalization System:

- **Persona-Based Pain Points**: Tailored to CFO, Facilities, or Sustainability priorities
- **Company-Specific Context**: References capital projects, infrastructure upgrades
- **Conversational Tone**: Natural language refined by two-stage AI process
- **Rapport Alignment**: Subject line themes match email opening sentences
- **No Sales Pitch**: Focus on relationship-building, not product pushing

## Three Email Series Structure

### Email #1: The Personalized Introduction
- **Goal**: Show you've done your homework + surface a relevant problem
- **Length**: 75-100 words maximum
- **CTA**: Simple yes/no question
- **Timing**: Day 1

### Email #2: The Follow-Up
- **Goal**: Resurface the first email with brevity
- **Length**: 30-50 words maximum
- **CTA**: Restate the original question
- **Timing**: 3 days after Email #1

### Email #3: The Breakup
- **Goal**: Pattern interruption + last-ditch effort
- **Length**: 50-75 words maximum
- **CTA**: Give them an easy out + humor
- **Timing**: 3-4 days after Email #2

### Subject Line Rules

**DO**:
- ‚úÖ Keep it 30-50 characters (mobile optimization)
- ‚úÖ Use first name when possible
- ‚úÖ Reference specific, relevant details
- ‚úÖ Create curiosity without being clickbaity
- ‚úÖ Test using "+" to break up subject lines

**DON'T**:
- ‚ùå Mention Metrus in the subject (save for body)
- ‚ùå Use sales language ("Free," "Limited time," "Offer")
- ‚ùå Ask for meetings in subject line
- ‚ùå Use all caps or excessive punctuation
- ‚ùå Exceed 50 characters

## Usage Guidelines

### Implementation Workflow

**Step 1: Segment Your Leads**
```
Tier 1 (Top 3%):
- Manual research (20+ minutes per contact)
- Hand-write each email using personalization fields
- Use hyper-personalized subject lines

Tier 2/3 (Next 17%):
- Light research (5 minutes per contact)
- Use templates with dynamic variables
- Semi-personalized subject lines
```

**Step 2: Gather Personalization Data**

Use your enrichment system to populate:
- `{{Rapport_Summary}}` - Professional recognition
- `{{Rapport_Summary_3}}` - Company-specific project
- `{{Summary_Why_Care}}` - Pain point connection
- `{{Similar_Hospital}}` - Social proof example
- `{{Achievement}}` - Recent accomplishment
- `{{Specific_Project}}` - Infrastructure project

**Step 3: Customize Per Persona**

Choose the template version that matches their detected persona:
- **Version 1** ‚Üí CFO, VP Finance, Finance Director
- **Version 2** ‚Üí Director Facilities, Maintenance Director, VP Plant Operations
- **Version 3** ‚Üí Energy Manager, Sustainability Director, Environmental Director

**Step 4: Send Three-Email Series**

```
Day 1:  Email #1 (Personalized Introduction)
Day 4:  Email #2 (Follow-Up) - if no reply
Day 7-8: Email #3 (Breakup) - if no reply
```

**Include Other Touchpoints**:
- Day 2: LinkedIn connection request
- Day 3: Phone call attempt
- Day 5: LinkedIn InMail (if connected)
- Day 6: Phone call attempt #2

---

### A/B Testing Strategy

**Test These Variables**:

1. **Subject Lines**:
   - Campaign 1 vs Campaign 2 vs Campaign 3 vs Campaign 4
   - First name vs no first name
   - Short (<30 chars) vs Medium (30-50 chars)

2. **Body Copy**:
   - Rapport_Summary position (beginning vs middle)
   - Summary_Why_Care phrasing ("I'm sure" vs "I imagine")
   - Social proof inclusion (with vs without peer hospitals)

3. **CTAs**:
   - "Would that be worth a conversation?"
   - "Is that something you'd want to explore?"
   - "Would that interest you?"

4. **P.S. Usage**:
   - With P.S. vs without P.S.
   - Social proof P.S. vs No-pressure P.S.

5. **Timing**:
   - 3-day cadence vs 4-day cadence
   - Tuesday/Thursday send vs Monday/Wednesday send

## Conclusion

### Remember: The Goal is a Response, Not a Meeting

**These emails work because they**:
1. ‚úÖ Show you've done your research (personalization)
2. ‚úÖ Address real pain points (relevance)
3. ‚úÖ Offer clear value (zero CapEx, guaranteed results)
4. ‚úÖ Make it easy to reply (yes/no questions)
5. ‚úÖ Remove pressure (P.S. clauses, humor, easy outs)
6. ‚úÖ Stand out from generic outreach (pattern interruption)

**The Formula**:
```
Personalization + Relevance + Brevity + Humor + Easy CTA = Response
```

**Success Metrics to Celebrate**:
- Any reply (yes, no, or later) is a win
- Opens and clicks show engagement
- "Not right now" replies are future pipeline
- "No" replies help your sender reputation

**Test, Learn, Iterate**:
- A/B test subject lines and body copy
- Track what works by persona
- Refine based on your unique audience
- Keep emails under 100 words
- Always provide an easy out
